Not My Body

In South Korea there is a constant lingering pressure for women and men to conform to societal expectations of appearance. Beneath the glitter and glam of makeup and beauty there lies the never-ending glorification of certain facial features. This societal obsession of achieving physical perfection is toxic and harms individualsâ€™ self-esteem.

This design work exhibits the interaction between photography and typography and how they can work to enhance and create new meanings. Initally, separate collections of portrait photographs and typographic posters were created with the intention of depicting the ambiguity of beauty shaped by South Korean culture. These elements were then combined and transformed to portray the underlying messages of harm that can come with unrealistic social standards. As South Korean trends continue to become more popular, it is a necessity to bring awareness to the fact that these beauty standards are simply a dangerous fantasy.
