Face Perfection

As a Korean-American designer, I am interested in exploring subjects drawn from one of these two cultures, or both. The blend of Korean and American cultures and traditions, among other things, offer up a rich and expansive visual and verbal vocabulary from which to work. A topic of great concern and interest is the imposition of outrageous and ultimately toxic beauty standards imposed on South Koreanswomen, men, girls, boys. The result is a kind of one-dimensional identity, leading to a culture that is neither itself nor any other; where difference and nuance are sooner scorned than celebrated. It's alarming, to say the least.

Initially, this rendition of the beauty box might be aesthetically pleasing to the eye. However, a deeper inspection brings to light the dangers of society's beauty standards. It raises the question: what toxic beauty standards does your culture hold against you?
