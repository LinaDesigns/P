Design Photography

Line/Point/Movement This project captures examples of three elemental design principles found in everyday objects in our surroundings. To isolate the raw formal (rather than symbolic) manifestation of each principle while engaging composition and framing skills.

Form/Image This project captures characteristics of specific three-dimensional paper forms. The process was divided into two segments: model-making and studio photography.

Objects This project captures key formal qualities of two objects. It explores how control of light and composition enable designers to visually describe shape, material, behavior, function, or mood/atmosphere associated with three-dimensional objects.

Visual Diary This visual diary captures a series of engagingly composed and properly exposed images that convey four provided themes: architecture, light/reflection, materiality, and letterform. The goal is to study, experiment, and make observations in an effort to cultivate the environment from which interesting ideas develop.
